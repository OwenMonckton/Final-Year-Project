Rockets Explained

Here you can input values into this table and get the calculations of how the engine would perform with those values.

Thrust How much thrust the engine produces in KN
Mass Flow The mass flow of the exhaust in kg/s
Exhaust Velocity The exhaust Velocity of the engine in Km/s
Specific Impulse (Isp) The specific impulse of the engine in seconds

This table works off 2 equations. Thrust = Mass flow X Exhaust Velocity, and Specific Impulse (Isp) = Exhaust Velocity X 9.81. Thrust is of course how much force the engine can generate at max throttle. Specific impulse is a measurement of efficiency. It tells you how efficient an engine is by how long it can produce a set amount of thrust given a set fuel load. So for example, if an engine has 100 seconds of Specific Impulse that means it can produce 1kg of thrust for 100 seconds from 1kg of fuel. So the larger the specific impulse, the more efficient the rocket engine.

To achieve such high specific impulse you have to accelerate your exhaust gasses extremely fast while at the same time not shooting out heavy amounts of exhaust gasses. This is why nuclear hydrogen engines are so efficient. They can super heat gasses hotter then combustion making the gasses super fast and by using hydrogen (which is the smallest element and lightest gas) they have a very light mass flow. They can accelerate smaller and lighter gasses faster. They can have specfic impulses upwards of 900 seconds compared to combustion that can only reach a peak of 450!
